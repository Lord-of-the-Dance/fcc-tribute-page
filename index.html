Marcus Tullius Cicero

A brilliant orator, whose ideas on life and philosophy are influential even today, over 2000 years later.

Timeline

  • 106 BC - Born in Arpinum
  • 90-88 BC - Campaigned in the Social War under Sulla, though he had no taste for military life
  • 87 BC - Studied Philosophy under Philo of Larissa, the head of the Platonic Academy
  • 80 BC - Takes on his first major public case as a lawyer: the defense of Sextus Roscius for the charge of patricide
  • 75 BC - Served a quaestorship in western Sicily, prosecuting a governor (Gaius Verres) during his time there, greatly pleasing the local populace
  • 63 BC - After ascending the Cursus Honorum, is elected Consul. During this time, he would famously thwart the Cataline Conspiracy, and put all conspirators to death, controversally without trial
  • 60 BC - Invited by Julius Caesar to be the fourth member of what would become the first Triumvirate and refused, believing correctly that it would undermine the Republic
  • 58 BC - Clodius, a newly elected Tribune of the Plebs, used Triumvirate backing to exile Cicero from Rome. Cicero left for Thessalonica, during which time his house was demolished and his reputation in shambles
  • 57 BC - Returned to Rome after a Senate vote recalled him from exile. He was received with cheering crowds of seemingly fickle citizens
  • 51 BC - Served as proconsul of Cilicia, quelling unrest and greatly improving the condition of its cities
  • 49 BC - Favored Pompey in the Civil War between him and Caesar. Pardoned afterwards by Caesar
  • 44 BC - Asked by Brutus to restore the Republic immediately after the assassination of Julius Caesar
  • 43 BC - Murdered under the orders of the Second Triumvirate, despite arguments from Octavian. Cicero's head and hands were both nailed to posts and displayed in the Forum, the only victim of these proscriptions to be treated in such a way, due to his antagonistic relationship with Antony
